About Joey & Koala

The app works by letting senior citizens sign up to request pre-defined tasks. The volunteer can respond to these tasks and book autonomous vehicles to incorporate volunteering into their work, study and life routine.

Problem Area

AVs are poised to restructure much of how the current world funcitons. With our design we wanted to develop a product that could take advantage of the potential for AVs to improve lives for the disadvantaged. This drew us towards areas such as post-natural-disaster reconnaissance, We decided to focus on addressing problems faced by senior citizens as we both had elderly parents and saw it was a sector with rapidly growing issues.

Desire to remain independent

In search of a problem within this sector, we conducted broad semi-structured interviews with a range of backgrounds in the senior citizens sector, including Australian and Korean elders living at home, in aged care communities and fully serviced apartments. One recurring theme struck us as most interesting: that senior citizens are and want to stay self sufficient but struggle with a few key tasks. This became the impetus for our product, Joey and Koala.

Lofi Wireframing

We finalised our exploratory designs trying to make a clear sense of direction in our UI flows and create a consistent visual language. User Testing Volunteers

For our volunteers, we tested users within the age rage of 20-40, and those that were typically quite active with technology and keen on using smartphones and devices. We tested a variety of users and the feedback we received was extremely valuable and allowed us to iterate on our prototypes effectively - e.g. larger text, greater clarity in the user interface. Our testees suggested the addition and removal of features, as well as providing general feedback on the concept and usability of the prototypes.

User Testing Senior Citizens

We tested the senior citizen focused prototype on three participants at The Manors of Mosman and from this realised we needed to take somewhat of a concept pivot. Due to poor views of AVs from senior citizens we shifted the focus of AV transport to the younger, more open-minded volunteers. Additionally, when 2/4 of the participants refused to use our prototype on mobile we realised it was worthless trying to design for the techno-phobic and instead focused on the senior citizens who are trying to integrate themselves by developing our wireframes on Ipad.

Final Design - Koala

For the final design of Joey, whilst it was progressively iterated from the original lo-fi wireframes and user testing, inspiration was also taken from apps such as Airbnb and Uber in regards to the autonomous vehicle element as well as task assessment ensuring the users to be proactive in a social and rewarding way. Additionally, the visual design of the app was designed to symbolise eucalyptus leaves linking directly to the names of Koala and Joey, as well as maintaining an appealing and professional feel to the app overall.
The screens These are just a handful of the screens that can be seen on the Joey application, however there is a clear consistency between all of the screens, familiar buttons, button locations and overall the impression from users is that Joey is a fully functional app that they would be able to navigate easily.

Final Design - Koala

For our final designs we used animations to enhance the sense of progression from completing a task by moving input to task descriptions seen at the top of the bin sign up process. We aimed to provide clear feedback at each step of a task to accommodate for the lowered working memory of senior citizens. Visually the app follows the same pallet as Joey, inspired by eucalyptus and uses the Muli font which we kept to a minimum of 24 px on high contrast backdrops to ensure our designs were accessible to the visually impaired.
